South Kalimantan: The Land of Lambung Mangkurat and the Meratus Mountains

South Kalimantan, located in the southeastern part of Borneo Island, is a province with a rich history, diverse cultures, and unique natural landscapes. From the bustling cities to the majestic Meratus Mountains and the unique floating markets, South Kalimantan offers a fascinating blend of experiences.

Historical Significance and the Banjar Sultanate:

South Kalimantan's history is deeply intertwined with the Banjar Sultanate, which played a significant role in the region's development:

  • Banjar Sultanate: This sultanate, which existed for centuries, influenced the region's culture, politics, and trade.
  • Lambung Mangkurat: The legendary figure of Lambung Mangkurat is an important part of Banjar folklore and history.
  • Historical Sites: Several historical sites in South Kalimantan provide insights into the region's past.

Cultural Diversity and Traditions:

South Kalimantan is home to a diverse population with various ethnic groups and cultural traditions:

  • Banjar People: The Banjar people are the dominant ethnic group in South Kalimantan, with their own language, customs, and traditions.
  • Dayak Groups: Several Dayak groups, such as the Dayak Meratus and Dayak Bakumpai, also inhabit the province, contributing to its cultural diversity.
  • Traditional Arts and Crafts: South Kalimantan has various traditional arts and crafts, including woven textiles (kain sasirangan), wood carving, and traditional dances.

Natural Beauty and Landscapes:

South Kalimantan is characterized by diverse natural landscapes:

  • Meratus Mountains: These mountains run through the province, creating stunning highland scenery and biodiversity.
  • Lowlands and Wetlands: The province also has extensive lowlands and wetlands, supporting unique ecosystems.
  • Rivers and Lakes: Several rivers and lakes are important for transportation, irrigation, and local communities.

Unique Floating Markets (Pasar Terapung):

One of South Kalimantan's most unique attractions is its traditional floating markets, where traders sell goods from boats on the rivers.

South Kalimantan: A Journey Through Culture, Nature, and History

South Kalimantan offers a rich tapestry of cultural traditions, natural beauty, and historical significance. Here's a deeper look:

Historical Perspective

Banjar Sultanate and its Legacy: The Banjar Sultanate's influence is still felt in South Kalimantan's culture, traditions, and historical sites.

Cultural Heritage

Banjar and Dayak Cultures: The blending of Banjar and Dayak cultures creates a unique cultural identity for the province.

Traditional Arts and Crafts: Sasirangan fabric, traditional dances, and other crafts are important parts of South Kalimantan's cultural heritage.

Natural Environment and Unique Features

Meratus Mountains and Biodiversity: The Meratus Mountains are a significant natural feature, supporting diverse ecosystems and biodiversity.

Floating Markets (Pasar Terapung): These unique markets are a major tourist attraction and a vital part of local trade and culture.

Economic Potential:

South Kalimantan's economy is supported by:

  • Mining: Mining, particularly coal, is a major economic sector.
  • Agriculture: Agriculture, including rubber, palm oil, and fisheries, also plays a significant role.
  • Trade and Services: Trade and services, particularly in Banjarmasin and Banjarbaru, are important economic drivers.

South Kalimantan Today

South Kalimantan is focused on developing its economy, particularly in the mining, agriculture, and tourism sectors, while preserving its rich cultural and natural heritage. The province is also working on improving infrastructure and promoting sustainable development.
